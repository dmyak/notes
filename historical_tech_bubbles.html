Technology Peak Period The Promise What Actually Happened Investor Outcome Society Outcome
Textile Mechanization (UK) 1760s 1760s–1790s Powered spinning/weaving would deliver vast productivity; fortunes for mill promoters. Capital rush into mills; many early firms failed or were absorbed; intense price competition. Frequent bankruptcies and consolidations; uneven returns for early shareholders. Kick‑started the factory system; dramatic drop in cloth prices; foundation of the Industrial Revolution.
Canals (UK) 1790s 1790s (Canal Mania) Transform inland transport; stable toll monopolies. Speculative boom; duplication and cost overruns; some canals never profitable. Large losses for late entrants; many issues diluted or restructured. Cheaper bulk transport; enabled coal/iron regions to flourish.
Steamships 1820s 1820s–1850s Timetabled ocean travel; domination of global trade routes. High capex; fierce competition; many lines failed or merged until scale achieved. Mixed to poor until consolidation; survivors prospered. Reliable long‑distance travel; knitted world commerce together.
Canals (US/UK) 1820s 1820s–1840s Monopoly toll revenues connecting cities and farms. Overbuilding; railroads soon displaced many routes within decades. Most bankrupt; shareholders wiped out. Boosted commerce; created connected cities.
Railways (UK) 1840s 1840s (Railway Mania) Capture all freight and passenger flows across Britain. Duplicate lines and scams; ~50% of projects never profitable. ~⅓ of capital lost; long workouts and mergers. Created modern Britain’s integrated market.
Telegraph 1840s 1840s–1860s Instant nationwide communication; toll monopolies. Overlapping networks; price wars; eventual monopoly consolidation (e.g., Western Union). Many start‑ups failed; late investors hurt in shakeouts. First real‑time communications network.
Railways (US) 1850s 1850s–1870s Open the West; land grants make investors rich. Overbuilding, rate wars, scandals; repeated bankruptcy waves (e.g., 1873). Multiple wipeouts before consolidation. Unified national economy.
Electric Lighting/Power 1880s 1880s–1890s Electrify cities; monopoly franchises. Fierce AC/DC competition; speculative utilities; rollups. Mixed; many local companies failed or were absorbed. Mass urban electrification.
Bicycles 1890s 1890s New mass personal transport; enduring premium margins. Boom → oversupply → price collapse by late 1890s. Heavy losses for late‑stage manufacturers. Mass mobility; pressure for better roads.
Automobiles 1910s 1915–1929 Every American will own a car; thousands of manufacturers. Shakeout; by 1929 top 3 controlled ~75%. 100+ companies → a handful; many wiped out. Created suburbs and modern logistics.
Electricity Holding Companies 1920s 1920s Utility holding pyramids create monopoly power and secure dividends. Highly leveraged pyramids collapsed in 1930–32; regulation followed (PUHCA). Huge losses for public investors. Universal electrification preserved/expanded under regulation.
Radio / RCA 1920s 1920s Wireless dominates communications; endless audience growth. Crash 1929–32; profits concentrated in a few networks. Peak buyers lost ~90%+ in the bust. Created mass media culture.
Airlines 1960s 1960s–1970s Jet age = goldmine for carriers. Deregulation → fare wars; restructurings. Most carriers bankrupt at least once. Democratized air travel.
Personal Computers 1980s 1980s–1990s PC on every desk = monopolistic profits. Commoditization; price collapse; software captured the value. Many hardware names vanished; a few software/platform winners. Computerized the world.
Biotech (first wave) 1980s 1980s–1990s Recombinant DNA will rapidly cure major diseases. Long development cycles; high trial failures; a few blockbusters. Boom–bust cycles; index‑level underperformance for years. Foundational therapies; biotech industry established.
Telecom Fiber / Carriers 1990s 1996–2001 Infinite bandwidth demand; 'picks and shovels'. Massive overbuild; transit prices –90%; frauds and mega‑bankruptcies. WorldCom, Global Crossing bankrupt, many equities to zero. Enabled modern internet.
Internet 1.0 1990s 1999–2001 Eyeballs = revenue; 'land grab'. Revenue models failed; cash burn. NASDAQ –78%; many dot‑coms died. Changed commerce forever.
Solar Panels 2000s 2008–2011 Peak oil + climate = infinite demand. Chinese competition → overcapacity; famous bankruptcies. Solyndra, SunEdison bankrupt; equity indexes fell hard. Now among the cheapest new energy sources.
Ride Sharing 2010s 2010s Replace car ownership; monopoly profits. Permanent fare wars; regulatory friction; slow path to profits. Long periods of drawdowns post‑IPO. Transformed urban mobility.
Cannabis 2010s 2018–2021 Legalization unlocks massive TAM. Oversupply; commoditization; regulatory hurdles. Most issues down 80%+ from peaks. Legalization progressing; new tax base.
SPACs 2020s 2020–2021 Democratize IPOs; find hidden gems. Poor governance; post‑merger underperformance. De‑SPAC indexes fell 70–80%+; many below $10. Some good companies financed; much capital misallocated.
Crypto/Web3 2020s 2020–2022 Decentralize everything; new financial system. Speculation; limited mainstream utility to date; major failures. Severe boom‑bust; large drawdowns for many tokens/equities. Blockchain tools developing; institutional rails emerging.
EVs 2020s 2020–2023 Every car electric by 2030. Demand slower than forecasts; infrastructure bottlenecks. Several OEM/EV equities down 60–90% from peaks at points. Adoption continues; tech improving.
AI (GenAI wave) 2020s 2023–? Transform every business; permanent margins. Massive capex; unclear monetization across the stack (so far). TBD — dispersion likely between platform and apps. TBD — productivity & safety trade‑offs.
